UST Museum exhibit features ‘adult coloring books’; stresses power of colors

Delving in a new exploration that teaches and communicates the value of arts to the public, the University of Santo Tomas (UST) Museum features works from “adult coloring books” in a month-long exhibit.

Titled “Color More, Stress Less,” the exhibition seeks to emphasize the importance of colors and coloring in this seemingly stressful time. It opened last April 11 and will run until May 6, 2016 at the UST Museum, España, Manila. “The UST Museum displays works not just to showcase but to share knowledge and experiences along the line of arts and culture,” UST Museum Director Fr. Isidro Abaño, O.P. said.

The previous year showed the phenomenon of interest in coloring books for adults with more people working on intricate illustrations using colored pencils and pens as materials.

“To better convey our message to the public, we might as well use what is already available and understandable to the present audience,” Fr. Abaño said. “Communication is two ways—giving and receiving. Now, many people are interested about coloring books because of its sudden popularity. So if you do something like this exhibit, you are already a step closer to communicating with them.”

Displayed in the exhibit were works by members of the group Coloring Book for Adult Philippines, which include Gilda Loja, Cathy Lasam-Ballo, Vermailene Barrios, Jacqueline Simpao-Callanta, Celeste Lecaroz-Aceron, Mike Torres, Red de Leon, Christian de Jesus, Ayn Descalsote, Ana Lourdes Aceron, Melissa Bautista-Paras, and Bianca Paras.

Colors and the body

According to Fr. Abaño, who is a colorist himself, coloring affects one’s psyche and gives an experience of lessening the stresses in life because it is enjoyable. This was expounded in two lecture-conferences held last March 12 at the Civil Law Auditorium, where UST Department of Psychology chair Prof. Ma. Claudette Agnes, Ph.D., talked about the “Psychology of Colors.” She is also the chair of the Commission on Higher Education Technical Committee of Psychology. Meanwhile, Prof. Grace Brillantes-Evangelista, Ph.D., chair of the Psychological Association of the Philippines Clinical Division, lectured on “Embracing Well- being through Arts.”

“Life today seems to be more complicated. They say technology is supposed to help us but technology has in a way made life a little bit stressful. That is why, perhaps, many engage in this activity. The repetitive strokes of the hand while coloring offer a relaxing and calming effect. It is in a way hypnotic,” Fr. Abaño said.

Fr. Abaño added that adult coloring books became appealing to many, especially to non-professional artists, because of the absence of pressure as to deadline, the choice of subject, and coloring techniques.

“Whenever I travel, I bring with me a small bag of coloring materials and a coloring book. When the flight is delayed, which is more often than not, or while in the airplane or train, you hardly notice the time. Coloring can be a way of passing time enjoyably and avoiding the manifestations of stress coming out of you,” he said.

Colors and cultures

Since time immemorial, people have considered colors to have a spectrum of cultural and religious symbolisms. Colors have affected peoples’ perspectives in their daily living.

“Look at the Buddhist sarong. It is orange because it exemplifies meditative awareness and spiritual enlightenment,” Fr. Abaño said. He mentioned that golden yellow, the color of the Vatican and UST, epitomizes the brightness of divinity and is “meant for God.”

Colors are also assistive in carrying out processes like how bright colors hasten the recovery of the sick and in the case of Fr. Abaño, colors aid him in prayer. “When I worked on the image of the Virgin Mary, every time I placed a dot, I prayed for somebody. If the Rosary has beads to aid the devout, I considered the dots in the artwork in the same way,” Fr. Abaño said.

Helping people gain peace and share experiences of joy were Fr. Abaño’s personal objectives in putting up the exhibit, saying that “If I have something like this that brings good, you might as well try it and maybe it will also be good for you.”

“Whatever joyful experiences one attains from this simple activity can possibly be shared to others. I hope we can send this message to many,” Fr. Abaño said.#Alexis Ailex C. Villamor, Jr.

UST, St. Mary’s University, London forge partnership

As a step towards establishing stronger ties and further amplifying its internationalization thrust, the University of Santo inked its 114th Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) this time with St. Mary’s University (SMU), Twickenham, London, the oldest Catholic university in the United Kingdom. With Dr. Francis Campbell, Vice Chancellor representing SMU, and Rev. Fr. Richard G. Ang, O.P., Acting Rector representing UST, the signing of the MOU recognized UST as SMU’s essential partner in the Asia-Pacific Region rendering it as one of its six global partners.

UST Office of International Relations and Programs Director Prof. Lilian J. Sison, Ph.D. and SMU Head of Partnerships and Global Enterprise Dr. David Reggio served as witnesses.

The Church and State Relationship

Prior to the MOU signing, Campbell delivered a lecture underscoring the persistent necessity to affiliate the social teachings of the Catholic Church with the demands of the present society. The lecture, “The Church and the Modern World”, was the second in the Diplomats’ Lecture Series of the University of Santo Tomas.

Campbell upheld that “faith is a central dimension of the state” rendering it as a groundwork for the ethical and political dimensions of the state in all of its endeavors. He also stressed the notion that a “ruptured relationship between faith, reason, and society” can result to a chaotic society leading to a drying up of solidarity, cultural relativism and healthy secularism.

In highlighting religion instilled with a public role, Campbell also espoused that the growing religious complexities prompted by the church and state relation can only be solved through the privatization of religion.

For Campbell, faith is reflected not just in the Church but also throughout the social order. It is in the society where one can find the collective signs of an animated faith — signs that at once can alter the grander social order and also support the individual and distinctive growth in virtue and devoutness. In his lecture, Campbell also discussed the fitting approach to faith. He emphasized that “faith is a product of various historical contexts and philosophical underpinnings.”

In acknowledging the multiplicity of religion, Campbell argued that religion must be viewed within the context and lenses of a pluralist methodology, as it is imbued with a “respected distinctiveness”, where faiths can engage in different autonomies upholding equilibrium in the society and provide solutions to contemporary cultures and democracy.

An Avenue for Cross-Cultural Experiences

During the press conference that followed the lecture and MOU signing, UST Office of International Relations and Programs Director Prof. Lilian J. Sison, Ph.D. commended the partnership with SMU as it will truly fortify the internationalization thrust of UST and students will be greatly exposed to the global environment.

“We welcome this partnership with St. Mary’s University because it will be another destination of our students,” she added.

In support of what Sison mentioned, Campbell also said that his long-term goal in this partnership is that of an exchange because it is in the exchange where an educational and cultural transfer can transpire.

"It comes with the history of institutions. We approach education in different ways but our common ground is the Catholic university network," Campbell said. He emphasized that the partnership is also a great means of showing how religion can engage the public sphere.

Through the said alliance, the SMU leader also said that the two Catholic universities, with religion as their springboard in achieving their goals, would be able to break the notion of religion as an element that is situated in the periphery effecting pluralism.

Campbell ended by accentuating that the partnership is a great opportunity for the Catholic identity to go back to its roots and foundations, for the enrichment of relationships, academic and cultural exchange, inciting camaraderie and unity for the two oldest Catholic Universities in the globe.

US Ambassador to PH headlines UST’s 3rd Diplomats’ Lecture Series

Centering on the privileges and opportunities afforded by a career in diplomacy and foreign service, United States (US) Ambassador to the Philippines Philip Goldberg shared his experiences and functions as a diplomat during the University of Santo Tomas’ (UST) 3rd Diplomats’ Lecture Series last April 27 at the Paredes Bldg., UST, Manila.

“One of the functions of foreign service is political diplomatic representation. Thus, wherever you are posted, you are responsible for representing the United States in various issues that would touch on the US and its relationship with the Philippines,” Ambassador Goldberg said.

According to the American diplomat, there are two trainings involved when preparing for diplomatic services—formal, which involves studying subjects “needed as one goes abroad” like international relations, history, and languages.

“Another is informal training. You need interest and curiosity in what’s going on in the world. You have to read newspapers and magazines,” said the Ambassador, who studied journalism in college.

As the US President’s official envoy to the country, Ambassador Goldberg said that in addition to the political aspects of his work, his position requires a myriad of other functions that delve on economic policy, public affairs, and even human resource management.

Understanding your own country and what your country and people aspire for are key factors in effectively understanding one’s role and representing the nation overseas, Ambassador Goldberg stressed.

Prior to his appointment to the Philippines in 2013, Ambassador Goldberg served as Assistant Secretary of the State for the Bureau of Intelligence and Research and was concurrently the Coordinator for Implementation of UNSC Resolution 1874 in North Ambassador Goldberg also served as Ambassador to Bolivia from 2006 to 2008, Chief of Mission in Pristina, Kosovo from 2004 to 2006, and Deputy Chief of Mission at the US Embassy in Santiago, Chile from 2001 to 2004.

When he was assigned as political-economic officer in Pretoria, South Africa to help prepare the country for democracy, Ambassador Goldberg shared that he was “lucky to have entered at a time when Nelson Mandela was coming in.”  "Over time, you have to accumulate knowledge that touch on different areas. As in other professions, you continuously learn more,” Ambassador Goldberg said.

UST and the US

Ambassador Goldberg said that UST and the US had important historical ties, which was forged during the Second World War.

“UST also has a very special place for us at the US Embassy and in the history of PH- US relations. People keep coming back to UST. In fact, in my office, I have a picture of the Americans being liberated here at Santo Tomas. So many of the relationships that were forged during that time remain today,” Ambassador Goldberg said.

The Ambassador added, “this university is older than any university I know of in the US, and this has a very special place in the pantheon of universities around the world.” Following the Ambassador’s lecture were questions from a panel of reactors composed of UST Graduate School Dean Marilu Madrunio, Faculty of Civil Law faculty member Atty. Enrique de la Cruz, and Faculty of Arts and Letters Student Council president Jan Dominic Castro.

Questions from students and faculty members in the audience were also entertained. UST Acting Rector Rev. Fr. Richard G. Ang, O.P. closed the gathering with hopes for deeper links between the University and the US Embassy in Manila.

“The partnerships UST has forged with American institutions have paved the way to many educational ties, various academic and cultural exchanges, sharing of best practices, active learning opportunities, and genuine friendship,” Fr. Ang said. “UST is committed to furthering these things and continue to aim for a stronger collaborative endeavors and dynamic cooperation.”

An artwork called “Fusion,” which was specially commissioned for the lecture series and made by internationally renowned artist Joe Datuin, was given to the Ambassador. The UST Diplomats’ Lecture Series was born in 2015 with the Deputy Head of Mission of the Embassy of France as inaugural speaker. The 2nd Diplomats’ Lecture Series, meanwhile, featured the Vice Chancellor of St. Mary’s University in London.#AV

USTV Awards fetes broadcast initiatives on responsible elections

This year’s USTV awards centered on recognizing the impact of social media on the youth’s worldviews and disposition influencing them to become judicious and sensible with the information presented to them by the different forms of media aiding them to champion the future of the Filipino family.

With the theme “Veritas in Caritate: The Youth Empowering the Family through Modern Media”, the 12th USTV Student Choice Awards, highlighted the substantial and sociopolitical role of media in the upcoming national elections. The yearly USTV Awards is organized by the Office of the Secretary General and Student Organizations Coordinating Council. It was held last April 21 at the UST Plaza Mayor.

In his opening remarks, UST Secretary General Rev. Fr. Winston F. Cabading, O.P., affirmed the comprehensiveness of the nature and agenda of the 12th USTv Students Choice Awards: “All in one package, the USTV XII covers the essential facets of Thomasian student formation namely, socio-cultural, socio- political, and moral-spiritual.”

This year’s awards had 33 categories and 104 nominees from GMA Channel 7 and GMA News TV, ABS-CBN, TV5 and CNN Philippines.

The 12th USTv Awards also introduced three new categories: the Students’ Choice of Public Announcement for the National Elections, Election Awareness Program, and Election Station ID.

ABS-CBN was hailed for its “Halalan 2016” election coverage as it was able to win the three new categories: Election Station ID: ABS-CBN News Ipanalo ang Pamilyang Pilipino; Choice of Public Service Announcement for National Elections and Election Awareness Progam: #Halalan2016.

ABS-CBN dominated the awards night. “Matanglawin: Justice for Pamana” bagged the award for Students’ Choice of Social Media Campaign for Environemental Protection. Evening news broadcast “TV Patrol” won the Students’ Choice of News Program.

Ten shows from the Kapamilya Network also won in the entertainment category: Students’ Choice of Magazine Program: KrisTV; Magazine Program Host: Kris Aquino (KrisTV); Sports Program: Dyan Castillejo; Comedy Program: Home Sweetie Home; Reality/Game Show: The Voice Kids;Talk Show: Tonight with Boy Abunda; Talk Show Host: Boy Abunda; Drama Program: On the Wings of Love.

GMA 7’s advocacy campaign “Dapat Tama” was the Choice of Social Media Campaign for Social-Political Development. “I-Witness” was acclaimed as the Students’ Choice of Public Affairs Program.

The Kapuso network also won two awards in the student leaders’ choice category and three in entertainment category: Student Leaders’ Choice of TV Program: State of the Nation with Jessica Soho; Choice of TV Network for Entertainment for News and Public Affairs: GMA News TV; Choice of Documentary Special: Reel Time; Youth Oriented Program: iBilib.

“Eat Bulaga”, the longest running noon-time variety show in the country, bagged the Students’ Choice of Variety Show Award which was received by Kapuso stars and tandem Jose Manalo and Wally Bayola dressed as the “Lolas” from Kalyeserye’s D’Explorer Sisters.

Recognizing the widespread use of social media by the youth, Atom Araullo, TV presenter and news reporter of ABS-CBN News and Current Affairs, was the Students’ Choice of Social Media Personality. Meanwhile, Maine Mendoza of the “AlDub” love team of Eat Bulaga’s Kalyeserye received the Special Citation for Social Media Phenomenon for AlDub.

McDonald’s bagged the award for Choice of TV Commercial for its advertisement “Tatanda at Lilipas din Ako” — a commercial tribute to parents for their hardwork and unending love for their children.

The 12th USTV awards night also showcased performances by the UST Symphony Orchestra, students from the UST Conservatory of Music, and ABS- CBN stars Anne Curtis and Alex Gonzaga who won the Students’ Choice of Variety Show Host (It’s Showtime) and Students’ Choice of Reality/Game Show Host (The Voice Kids), respectively.

More About OPA

The Office of Public Affairs was created in 1965 with the primary function of spearheading the publicity efforts of all faculties, colleges, departments and institutes of the University and taking charge of special publications such as souvenir programs, handbooks, leaflets and other related materials. The first director was Prof. Ernesto Franco..
