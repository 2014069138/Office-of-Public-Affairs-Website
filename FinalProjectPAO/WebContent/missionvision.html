      Mission


            The Office of Public Affairs commits itself to be a creative and dynamic partner of the University in communicating the University’s   identity concretized in its expressed mission and vision.

            It conveys messages to the University’s various stakeholders through the packaging of relevant information into functional and   interesting media forms.

            It is a corporate-oriented advocate of quality service to its clientele for sustained positive interpersonal relationship.

            As an enthusiastic initiator in building goodwill among its stakeholders, it aims to be a committed catalyst of change as an effective   center of interactive communication that is guided by the Gospel values and inspired by a constant pursuit of the Truth.


      Vision


            The Office of Public Affairs envisions itself in the next three years to promote a leadership of service in PR practice in an academic   setting through an empowered human resource trained to be multi-skilled and formed with Christian ethics that values excellence in   service and interpersonal relationships for a public relations program that includes:

  •         organization of special events aimed at the dissemination of goodwill and social consciousness;

  •         information dissemination and communication with internal and external publics directed towards brand and service promotion;

  •         a well-planned acquisition and preparation of the state of the art media tools that will ensure a most attractive and techno logically       fit packaging of the communication plan; and

  •         a focused and consistent communication of the defined University identity embodied in its mission and vision that is disseminated       not only to the University community but likewise to its external publics in Asia and the world at large.

More About PAO

The Office of Public Affairs was created in 1965 with the primary function of spearheading the publicity efforts of all faculties, colleges, departments and institutes of the University and taking charge of special publications such as souvenir programs, handbooks, leaflets and other related materials. The first director was Prof. Ernesto Franco..

- Office of Public Affairs
